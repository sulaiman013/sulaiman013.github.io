SQL-Based Data Analysis for Business Intelligence

Turning raw data into actionable insights through ad-hoc SQL queries

In today's data-driven business landscape, the ability to extract meaningful insights from raw data is invaluable. This project demonstrates how SQL can be leveraged to analyze consumer goods data and provide actionable business intelligence for Atliq Hardwares, a fictional computer hardware manufacturer with global operations.

Introduction to Atliq Hardwares and the Project Scope

Atliq Hardwares stands as a prominent player in the computer hardware industry, with a significant market presence both in India and international markets. Like many forward-thinking organizations, they recognize the critical importance of data-driven decision making to maintain competitive advantage. This project was designed as part of a hypothetical hiring challenge for junior data analysts, crafted by Tony Sharma, the Director of Data Analytics at Atliq Hardwares.

The core objective was to tackle various ad-hoc business requests using SQL queries to derive valuable insights from the company's database. The project demonstrates how data analysis can inform strategic business decisions across multiple domains including sales performance, product management, customer relations, and manufacturing costs.

Database Structure and Data Overview

The Atliq Hardware database comprises six interconnected tables that collectively provide a comprehensive view of the business operations:

Customer Dimension (dim_customer)

This table contains customer information including unique customer codes, names, platforms (e.g., "Brick & Mortar" or "E-Commerce"), distribution channels, geographical market data, and regional classifications.

Product Dimension (dim_product)

This table stores product details with unique product codes and categorizes them by division (like "P & A," "N & S," and "PC"), segment, category, product name, and variant.

Gross Price Facts (fact_gross_price)

Contains product pricing information across different fiscal years (2020 and 2021), linking product codes to their respective gross prices before deductions.

Manufacturing Cost Facts (fact_manufacturing_cost)

Tracks the total production costs for each product, including raw materials, labor, and overhead expenses.

Pre-Invoice Deductions Facts (fact_pre_invoice_deductions)

Documents the percentage of pre-invoice deductions applied to products for different customers and fiscal years.

Monthly Sales Facts (fact_sales_monthly)

Records monthly sales transactions with details on dates, product codes, customer codes, sold quantities, and fiscal years.

Key Benefit of SQL for Business Analysis

SQL's ability to join multiple tables and filter complex data allows analysts to answer specific business questions quickly without requiring extensive preprocessing or data transformation steps.

Ad-Hoc Business Requests Analysis

Let's explore each of the ten business requests that were addressed using SQL queries, examining both the technical approach and the business implications of the findings.

Request 1: Identifying Atliq Exclusive's APAC Markets

Business Objective: Determine the markets in which the customer "Atliq Exclusive" operates within the APAC region.

The SQL query filtered the dim_customer table to identify distinct markets where Atliq Exclusive operates within the APAC region. The results revealed that Atliq Exclusive has established a presence in eight APAC markets: Australia, Bangladesh, India, Indonesia, Japan, New Zealand, Philippines, and South Korea.

This information provides valuable insights into Atliq Exclusive's geographical footprint, which can inform regional marketing strategies, supply chain optimization, and potential market expansion opportunities.

Request 2: Product Portfolio Growth Analysis (2020-2021)

Business Objective: Calculate the percentage increase in unique products between fiscal years 2020 and 2021.

The analysis revealed a significant 36.33% increase in unique products from 245 in 2020 to 334 in 2021. This substantial growth indicates an aggressive product diversification strategy by Atliq Hardwares during this period.

For business stakeholders, this metric serves as a key indicator of product development momentum and portfolio expansion. Such rapid growth might suggest either a strategic push into new market segments or a response to emerging consumer demands.

Request 3: Product Distribution Across Segments

Business Objective: Provide a segment-wise breakdown of product counts in descending order.

The analysis categorized the product portfolio by segments, revealing that "Notebook" leads with 129 unique products, followed by "Accessories" (116), "Peripherals" (84), "Desktop" (32), "Storage" (27), and "Networking" (9).

This distribution clearly indicates Atliq's strategic focus on portable computing solutions, with notebooks and their accessories constituting the largest portion of their product portfolio. For business leaders, this insight helps evaluate whether the current product distribution aligns with market trends and company strategy.

Request 4: Segment Growth Analysis (2020-2021)

Business Objective: Identify which segment experienced the most significant increase in unique products between 2020 and 2021.

The follow-up analysis determined that the "Accessories" segment saw the most substantial growth, with 34 new unique products added in 2021. "Notebook" and "Peripherals" tied for second place with 16 new products each, followed by "Desktop" (15), "Storage" (5), and "Networking" (3).

This growth pattern suggests a strategic emphasis on expanding the accessories line, which often carries higher profit margins and complements the company's strong notebook segment. For product managers, this trend highlights potential areas for continued investment or realignment based on business objectives.

Request 5: Manufacturing Cost Extremes

Business Objective: Identify products with the highest and lowest manufacturing costs.

The analysis identified "AQ HOME Allin1 Gen 2" as the product with the highest manufacturing cost at $240.54, while "AQ Master wired x1 Ms" had the lowest cost at just $0.89.

This stark contrast in manufacturing costs across the product range provides valuable insights for cost management, pricing strategy, and profitability analysis. Products with exceptionally high manufacturing costs might require special attention to ensure they maintain acceptable profit margins.

Request 6: Top Discount Recipients in India (2021)

Business Objective: Identify the top 5 customers in India who received the highest average pre-invoice discounts during fiscal year 2021.

The analysis revealed that Flipkart received the highest average discount rate at 30.83%, followed closely by Viveks (30.38%), Ezone (30.28%), Croma (30.25%), and Amazon (29.33%).

This information is crucial for discount strategy evaluation. The similar discount levels across major e-commerce and retail chains suggest a standardized approach to large customer accounts in India. Sales managers can use this data to optimize discount strategies and assess their impact on profitability.

Request 7: Monthly Gross Sales Analysis for Atliq Exclusive

Business Objective: Generate a month-by-month report of gross sales for the customer "Atliq Exclusive" to identify performance patterns.

The analysis tracked Atliq Exclusive's gross sales across all months in both 2020 and 2021, revealing significant performance variations. November consistently emerged as the top-performing month in both years, with $15.23 million in 2020 and $32.25 million in 2021. March 2020 showed the lowest performance at just $0.77 million.

This monthly performance data provides essential insights into seasonality patterns and year-over-year growth. The consistent November peak suggests potential holiday season sales impact, while the dramatic improvement from March 2020 to March 2021 (from $0.77M to $19.15M) could indicate either market recovery post-disruption or successful strategic initiatives.

Request 8: Quarterly Sales Performance in 2020

Business Objective: Determine which quarter of 2020 achieved the highest total sales quantity.

The quarterly analysis for 2020 revealed that Q1 achieved the highest sales volume with 7,005,619 units sold, followed by Q2 (6,649,642), Q4 (5,042,541), and Q3 (2,075,087).

This quarterly distribution highlights significant seasonal variations in sales volume, with Q3 performing notably below other quarters. This pattern could inform inventory planning, marketing campaign timing, and production scheduling to align with expected demand fluctuations.

Request 9: Channel Contribution Analysis (2021)

Business Objective: Determine which sales channel contributed the most to gross sales in fiscal year 2021 and calculate percentage contributions.

The analysis identified "Retailer" as the dominant sales channel, accounting for 73.22% of gross sales ($1,924.2 million) in 2021. "Direct" sales contributed 15.48% ($406.7 million), while "Distributor" sales accounted for 11.31% ($297.2 million).

This channel distribution clearly demonstrates Atliq's heavy reliance on retail partners for revenue generation. For business strategists, this insight could prompt discussions about channel diversification, retailer relationship management, or potential optimization of underperforming channels.

Request 10: Top-Performing Products by Division (2021)

Business Objective: Identify the top 3 products in each division based on total sold quantity in fiscal year 2021.

The analysis identified the following top performers by division:

  • N & S Division: "AQ Pen Drive 2 IN 1" (701,373 units), "AQ Pen Drive DRC" (688,003 units), and another "AQ Pen Drive DRC" variant (676,245 units)
  • P & A Division: "AQ Gamers Ms" (428,498 units), "AQ Maxima Ms" (419,865 units), and another "AQ Maxima Ms" variant (419,471 units)
  • PC Division: "AQ Digit" (17,434 units), "AQ Velocity" (17,280 units), and another "AQ Digit" variant (17,275 units)

This product-level performance data helps identify star performers within each division, informing inventory management, marketing focus, and potential product development directions.

SQL Techniques Used in the Project

  • JOINs: Combining data from multiple tables
  • Window Functions: For ranking products and complex calculations
  • Common Table Expressions (CTEs): Breaking complex queries into manageable parts
  • Aggregate Functions: Calculating totals, averages, and percentages
  • CASE Statements: Handling conditional logic
  • String Manipulation: Dealing with text data

Key Insights and Business Implications

The comprehensive analysis of Atliq Hardwares' data has yielded several valuable business insights:

Product Strategy Insights

The significant 36.33% growth in unique products between 2020 and 2021, particularly in the Accessories segment, demonstrates an aggressive product diversification strategy. The notebook-heavy product portfolio indicates a clear strategic focus on portable computing solutions, aligned with broader industry trends toward mobility.

Sales and Distribution Insights

The analysis revealed the critical importance of the retail channel, which generated over 73% of gross sales in 2021. This highlights both a strength in retail partnerships and a potential vulnerability in channel concentration. The consistent seasonal patterns, with November showing peak performance, provide valuable guidance for inventory and marketing planning.

Customer Relationship Insights

The discount analysis for Indian customers revealed remarkably similar discount levels across major e-commerce and retail partners, suggesting a standardized approach to key accounts. This could be examined further to determine if more nuanced, performance-based discount structures might yield better results.

Manufacturing and Cost Insights

The extreme variation in manufacturing costs across different products (from $0.89 to $240.54) highlights the diverse nature of Atliq's product portfolio and the importance of product-specific margin management.

Conclusion and Future Directions

This SQL-based analysis of Atliq Hardwares' business data demonstrates the power of structured database queries in extracting actionable business intelligence. By systematically addressing ten diverse business questions, we've uncovered valuable insights across multiple business dimensions including market presence, product strategy, sales performance, cost management, and customer relationships.

For Atliq Hardwares, these insights could inform numerous strategic decisions, from market expansion priorities to product development focus areas, from channel optimization strategies to discount structure revisions. Most importantly, this analysis establishes a template for ongoing data-driven decision making, where business questions can be systematically translated into SQL queries to extract relevant insights.

In an increasingly competitive consumer goods landscape, this analytical capability represents a significant competitive advantage, enabling more informed, evidence-based decision making across all levels of the organization.
